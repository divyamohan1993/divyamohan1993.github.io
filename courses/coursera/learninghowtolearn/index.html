Learning How to Learn: Powerful mental tools to help you master tough subjects

A course by Deep Teaching Solutions on Coursera | Completed by Divya Mohan on June 14, 2022

This course gives the learners easy access to the invaluable learning techniques used by experts in art, music, literature, math, science, sports, and many other disciplines. We learned about how the brain uses two very different learning modes and how it encapsulates (“chunks”) information. We were also told about illusions of learning, memory techniques, dealing with procrastination, and best practices shown by research to be most effective in helping us master tough subjects.

Using these approaches, no matter what our skill levels are in topics we would like to master, we can change our thinking and change our life. If someone is already an expert, this peep under the mental hood will give them ideas for turbocharging successful learning, including counter-intuitive test-taking tips and insights that will help them make the best use of their time on homework and problem sets. If someone is struggling, they will see a structured treasure trove of practical techniques that walk them through what they need to do to get on track.

If you’ve ever wanted to become better at anything, this course will help serve as your guide. This course can be taken independent of, concurrent with, or prior to, its companion course, Mindshift. (Learning How to Learn is more learning-focused, and Mindshift is more career-focused.) A related course by the same instructors is Uncommon Sense Teaching.

Week 1

This module used metaphor and analogy to help simplify matters. We discovered several fundamentally different modes of thinking, and how we can use these modes to improve our learning. We were also introduced to a tool for tackling procrastination, given some practical information about memory, and discovered surprisingly useful insights about learning and sleep.

Week 2

In this module, we were told about chunks. Chunks are compact packages of information that our minds can easily access. We were told about how we can form chunks, how we can use them to improve our understanding and creativity with the material, and how chunks can help us to do better on tests. We also explored illusions of competence in learning, the challenges of overlearning, and the advantages of interleaving.

Week 3

In this module, we were told about two intimately connected ideas—procrastination and memory. i.e. Building solid chunks in long-term memory, (chunks that are easily accessible by your short-term memory) takes time. This is why learning to handle procrastination is so important. Finally, we were told about some of the best ways to access our brain’s most powerful long-term memory systems.

Week 4

In this module, we were told more about important ideas and techniques that will enhance our ability to learn. We also discovered how to more profitably interact with fellow learners, how to recognize our own strengths, and how to avoid the “imposter syndrome.” Fighter pilots and surgeons use checklists to help them with their critical duties—we can use a similar checklist to help us prepare for tests. Ultimately, we learned more about the joys of living a life filled with learning!

What is a Professional Certificate?

Professional certificates are credentials you can earn to show your competency in a specific skill. Typically, you receive your certification as a document stating that you have the professional training, education, and preparation to meet a specific set of criteria for your role. What is a Professional Certificate equivalent to?

Many professional certificates have the same curriculum as master's degrees in the same subject. Many other professional certificates offer the same courses as master's degrees in the same subject, but require the student to take fewer total courses to complete the program.
